The Legendary Nordic Jörmungandr

Myths

Lifting The Cat

  • The Norse God Thor encounters the giant king Utgaroa-Loki and must perform deeds for him. The most challenging: Thor must prove his strength. He displays his strength by lifting the Midgard Serpent Jörmungandr disguised by magic as a massive cat. He raises it high enough for only the cat's paws to touch the floor He later discovers it was indeed the serpent and he was able to raise it to almost touch the sky. If Thor would have lifted it completely off the ground, he would have altered the boundaries of the universe.

Thor's Fishing Trip

  • Thor and Jörmungandr encounter each other once more during Thor's fishing expedition with the giant Hymir. Despite Hymir's refusal to provide bait, Thor takes matters into his own hands by decapitating Hymir's largest ox. As they row to a familiar fishing spot of Hymir's, Thor insists on venturing further out to sea, against Hymir's objections. After preparing a strong line with an ox head as bait, Jörmungandr takes it and is hooked by Thor. As Thor pulls the serpent from the water, they confront each other, with Jörmungandr emitting poison. Hymir, overcome with fear, watches on. Just as Thor prepares to strike the serpent with his hammer, Hymir cuts the line, causing Jörmungandr to sink back into the depths. The tale, found in the Eddic poem Hymiskviða and skaldic poetry, varied in earlier versions where Thor successfully defeats the serpent. The story of Thor's encounter with Jörmungandr was a popular theme in Norse art, depicted on various picture stones such as the Altuna Runestone and the Ardre VIII image stone. These depictions often show Thor battling the serpent or its escape by the giant's action. The consistent portrayal of this myth across different artworks suggests its enduring popularity and preservation over several centuries before being recorded in written form in the Prose Edda around 1220.
